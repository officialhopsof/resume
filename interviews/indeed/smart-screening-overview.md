# 👋 Welcome!
This document provides an overview of the Smart Screening project, formerly known as Advanced Screening and Project Nexus. High-level context below, but the best way to navigate this doc is through tabs.
# 💡Quick Facts
Project Goal: automating information gathering, candidate evaluation, screening, and engagement all the way through to onboarding.
Key Customer Pain Points:
· Too many unqualified applications
· High recruiting team costs
· Overworked TA team
· Slow time to fill
Value Proposition:
· Hours of recruiter hours saved: Reduces phone screen time by asking dynamic open-ended questions based on information not found in screening or resumé.
· Best in class job seeker experience: High conversion for quality candidates at the top of the funnel.
· Customizable: Fully customizable fit-score based on the unique needs of the company
· ATS Integration: Candidate Summary Page integration is instant with IndeedApply. Ability to extend custom integration is available for larger employers.
· Compliance-first: Dedicated responsible AI team
· LLM Review: Review from TA professionals to ensure scoring is accurately capturing the nuance in hiring
Target Audience: Scaled employers with large recruiting teams who want to focus on quality candidates.

# FAQ
How are we going to monetize this thing?
We plan to bring to market as a software subscription purchasable on top of sponsored jobs and smart sourcing. We will help employers source their candidate pool when they need volume, and help them process their candidate pool when they need quality & hiring efficiency.
I thought most of this was majorly legally fraught - can we have a score? Edit the resumes? Automatically detect employment gaps?
As part of the subscription, employers will agree to our basic terms of service - just like they do for advertising today. These terms will include Indeed operating as an employment agency, enabling us to do what recruiters actually do today.
Which customers are we targeting here? Who is this really for?
The initial phases are focused exclusively on Indexed clients. We will allow all TPIA customers (those who use a third party ATS) to purchase, but not targeting below UMM+ since these features are focused on helping companies manage candidates on a large scale. Imagine having to process 1,000+ applicants in a single week, vs hiring a single manager for your local laundromat. However, we expect that small businesses will also benefit from the added insights of AI interviews, improved matching & relevance scores, and the time savings of automations even for low-volume hiring. The go-to-market & pricing will eventually reflect scale, and the user experiences will certainly be different, drawing attention to and enabling different feature suites based on customer needs.
Isn’t a lot of this the same thing we’re offering as part of the Talent Scout employer agent?
Will employers actually click through these and go to Indeed?

This all hinges upon having a really good product for IndeedApply screening and automations (stuff like CRMs already do well, like text messaging, interview scheduling, etc). What makes us think we can do this at scale?
This is the same set of product requirements we are banking on for Sponsored Jobs Premium anyway. Whatever risk we have here, we already incurred it in our committed plans for Premium. The main barrier to adoption for our automation products is that they need to handle all applies in order to be valuable to the employer.
Have we assessed that clients would buy a career site from Indeed? What makes us think this would have Product-Market Fit?

What if we roll out Nexus, and a bunch of current spenders adopt it, won’t we lose a bunch of sponsored job revenue?
Current employers who use IA and sponsored jobs do need the additional volume. Switching to Nexus is not going to get them additional volume.
New users to IA entirely will definitely have this risk, but only 25% of revenue comes from those today, and we can probably price it appropriately such that we have no risk (and only reward).
Much of our revenue growth should come from non-montizable jobs and accounts under current sponsored job offerings.
This is like dejavú. A year ago, didn’t we get excited about monetizing IA? What happened? Indeed Apply Monetization Strategy
Last year’s strategy was entirely about charging for things we already enabled for free (a paygating initiative). It had nothing to do with the added feature suite for quality & taking over more of the hiring flow through automated pre-screening processes. As such, the expected rev opportunity was extremely low (eg $20M).
We think the opportunity of monetizing jobs which do not need sponsorship & brand new value can be benchmarked to competitors at a much higher $ value
Why would we do this instead of driving adoption of our “Apply with Indeed” integration that already puts on us ATS-generated career sites?
AWI ATS adoption is much lower than IA ATS adoption, so we would substantially limit our TAM. Also, some users won't use the AWI button. If we owned the apply button, we'd essentially have visibility into all the career site applies in addition to the on-Indeed applies. Lastly, alternative providers are selling solutions anchored on career sites that work with ATSs. Not providing this relegates us to a more limited role in the employer’s stack.
Won’t ATSs think this is competitive?
Given the fragmentation of the ATS market, there’s not one answer to this question. However, there’s a strong market for HRTech companies that started with and specialize in optimizing career site and apply flow conversation rates. Paradox, Eightfold.ai, PhenomPeople, and many others integrate with the ATS and offer a better career site than the ATS. The ATSs often work with these players because they do not view the career site as a competitive advantage. It’s a commodity feature they need to have as part of their overall product offering, but it’s not a differentiating feature. Employers who use an ATS run RFPs to buy career sites that they intend to work with their ATSs.
Additionally, because we are selling this offering directly to employers and relying on existing Indeed Apply integrations, for the ATSs that believe this offering is competitive, the only option they would have is to de-activate their existing Indeed Apply integration. That is an employer hostile action they are unlikely to take. They may refuse to further enhance their Indeed Apply integrations and this is noted separately in the risks section.
Isn’t relying on ATS integrations really risky?
Relying on new ATS integrations is really, really risky. In fact, many of the integration teams across interoperability and automation prefer this strategy of taking the career site precisely because it removes new ATS integrations from the critical path for scaling. Instead, we will rely on existing Indeed Apply integrations that have been built and enhanced over a 15 year period. Additionally, this represents a unique advantage for Indeed. We are the only entity on the planet with the depth and breadth of apply flow integrations to enable us to offer this solution with built-in compatibility across so many ATSs. Most other entities need to build bespoke integrations per employer that activates their product. This does create a long term risk on the quality of our Indeed Apply integrations, but Interoperability and Indeed Apply have been working for the past 3 years to mitigate that risk for other reasons.
Why would employers buy this from us instead of from a different career site and automation vendor (e.g. Paradox)?
There are two key facts laid out at the top of the document that inform why an automation vendor like Paradox would not be able to successfully compete with this product package.
First, employers want all of their applicants to go through a single standardized application process, and second, Employers have a dropoff problem specifically with Indeed where jobseekers who complete an Indeed Apply for a job that has automation often refuse to participate in the automation steps (even if it’s only interview screening). We’ve heard from employers that the dropoff rate ranges from 30% to 80%.
Only an automation offering that exists in the step immediately after submitting an application on both the career site and indeed.com is effectively able to address the dropoff issue. We will not allow anyone direct access to the smart apply flow to insert automation tech that isn’t provided through Nexus if we implement this strategy.
As a corollary to our unique ability to address dropoff on the career site and on indeed.com, if Indeed Apply powers the application process on the career site and on indeed.com, all other quick apply offerings retain the dropoff problem and become non-standard, less compliant applications. Longer term (5+ years) that will work with the long term Indeed+ strategy of powering HRTech broadly.
You mentioned Indeed+, say more. How would this synergize?
In order for the I+ strategy to succeed they need ATSs and Job Boards to adopt at scale. In the US market in particular, there is reason to believe Indeed+ will struggle to scale. While there are some job boards and ATS that are willing to use our technology to power their career sites and job boards, there are not nearly as many as there are in the JP market (as Recruit has a stronger position relative to the JP market than Indeed to RoW). Additionally, this creates an additional dependency on convincing ATSs to integrate with new technology. Between the fragmentation of the market, competitive and strategic concerns of ATSs, and ongoing roadmap competition from new features (like AI), it will take a long time to move the market.
However, in a counterfactual world where the majority of applications for many employers already flow through Indeed technology, we can easily approach ATSs and Job Boards with a simple pitch.
For job boards - “Given the extra steps and associated dropoff when you send jobseekers to our career site (vs. our ability to power an on-Job Board apply flow), your jobseeker experience is worse and the ROI of spend on your site is diminished. Use our smart apply technology on your Job Board surface and those issues will vanish overnight.”
For ATSs (and career site builders) - “Our career site product is gaining steam with your clients as a result of the quality of our software and apply flow integrations, but we’re happy to share the tech with you to offer in a white-label fashion.” Either scenario is acceptable to Indeed because we maintain our ability to monetize the transition to post-apply processing and automation steps.
Another way to frame this is it is an early initiative to drive powering career sites through One Platform.
What about screener questions? Aren’t those automations?
Screener questions fall into a grey area. While they represent automated screening and rejection, they are also part of the application itself. Because of the structure of our Indeed Apply integrations, we likely cannot omit or monetize screener questions that originate from the ATS. However, if we have uniquely valuable screener questions created by Indeed (such as a commute map screener question), we could monetize those directly in this approach.
How will we make money on this?
One of the user problems we want to solve is a desire for effective automation across all of their applications. Today, Indeed monetizes the acquisition of applications. You pay us money and we will send more application volume to you. We have long wanted to charge further down-funnel for other activities (like interviewing in Hiring Events or even scheduling the interview). However, the automations themselves are commodities, and their monetization is by itself not interesting and is often dependent on their integration with the ATS.
If we pursue this strategy, we eliminate new integrations with the ATS from the critical path and more importantly we gain the opportunity to monetize the presentation of the automations to the jobseeker after they have submitted their application. We monetize our unique ability to present interview scheduling (Hiring Event RSVPs, Interview Now, etc.) to jobseekers. That plays far more to Indeed’s market strengths than trying to build a commodity integrations businesses.

# Overview
Nexus is a solution for recruiting teams managing high candidate volume and looking for quality over quantity. We go beyond traditional ATS platforms by automating the operational heavy lifting - candidate evaluation, scheduling, verification, and engagement - while giving teams full control over how “quality” is defined and measured. Nexus is a purchasable product independent of sponsored jobs which packages many existing and some new features.
What Recruiting Teams Get with Nexus
· One-page recruiting summaries for each candidate embedded directly in their ATS, showing match quality compared to the unique needs of the job, with current candidate status & links to deeper insights.
· The ability to define and update nuanced screening criteria & automation workflows at any time for any role, one-off or in bulk, and have new preferences collected and reflected in subsequent applications.
· Automation tools from evaluation to verification to scheduling, customizable to any desired level of control, available for any candidates - applying via Indeed, referral, or career page.
· Persistent access to the full applicant pool for every role, with tools to revisit or re-engage candidates anytime and refresh current status.
# Key Capabilities
Understands Hiring Intent Hiring managers describe what they’re looking for in their own words - no forms, dropdowns, or templates (Project Hinge, PRD). Nexus translates this input into a tailored search profile that reflects the employer’s real priorities, then uses it to evaluate and rank candidates.
Evaluates Candidates Based on Hiring Intent Every applicant is evaluated against the employer’s priorities - including complex concepts like employment consistency & interest in the role, and recruiters and hiring managers receive a fit score with clear explanations. We can leverage the apply & post-apply flows to ask job seekers questions when we need more information; soft skills and experience can be assessed through AI-driven interviews.
Performs Work and Credential Verification Work history, licenses, I-9s, and employment eligibility are verified automatically using third party providers.
Confirms Engagement and Automates Scheduling Nexus can handle outreach, confirm candidate interest, and schedule interviews before a recruiter ever picks up the phone.
Delivers Candidates to the ATS with Structured Dossiers Applicants are presented in the ATS with a standardized, one-page dossier showing verification status, employment gaps, soft skill alignment, and actionable next steps - at the top of the page before the resume. All within recruiters’ existing tools, eliminating the need to toggle between systems.
Refreshes Dossiers Automatically with Browser Extensions
As recruiters browse candidates in their ATS, Nexus stays in sync—refreshing fit scores, updating engagement signals, and flagging new information in real time. Browser extensions ensure the most current insights are always available, no context switching required.

# Smart Screening Overview and Goals (fka Nexus)
· Help employers solve the application quality problem by evaluating candidates on behalf of employers and delivering an Indeed Generated Summary along with the resume for applies
· Allow employers to find top candidates more quickly
· Allow employers to integrate with verifications and scheduling
· Allow employers to use automations
· Sell as a tier-based subscription
· Critical piece of the FY25 Quantity to Quality objective
· We will offer a basic Smart Screening product for free to all employers who turn on Third Party Indeed Apply (TPIA)
· We will offer an advanced Smart Screening product as an upsell that adds
· Ability to customize your match criteria
· License verifications
· Multi-job setup/management
· Quality-based automations inc. ability to message candidates and schedule interviews based on match scores
# Legal Clearance We Have for Nexus
· Applicant scoring and use of employer’s private criteria
· We are generating an Indeed Generated Score and summary for candidates, pre-pending this to the resume, and sending to the ATS. This information is not shown to the jobseeker explicitly and does not become part of their profile
· We will evaluate and report to employers on private criteria they may wish to screen on (e.g. job hopping, employment gaps)
· These are both areas that represent a change in stance from Indeed’s historical legal perspective
· We will sell Smart Screening / Nexus products under the Indeed Inc legal entity, and we will not require clients to sign an agency agreement
· Will add AI interviews into the scoring function
· Will add automations and other additional features
· Legal + RAI process for scoring and prompt iterations
· Scoring - initial bias audits by RAI and iteration reviews via PDBE (must pass to move forward with iterations)
· Explanations - checked against RAI counterfactuals
· LEAPS - we’re working on the requirements we’re aware of - JS accommodations and data retention policy in particular
· NYC audit - this is subject to legal deciding we need a 3rd party bias audit and choosing a vendor
· In the event of bias detected in prod
· Follow some version of the remediation plan (section 8 here)
