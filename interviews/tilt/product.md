# Tilt - Engineering Manager Product Interview Questions

## Product Strategy & Vision

1. **How do you balance technical excellence with speed to market in a startup environment?**
   - What frameworks do you use to make these tradeoffs?
   - Can you give an example of when you prioritized speed over perfection?

2. **Tell me about a time when you disagreed with a product decision. How did you handle it?**
   - What was the outcome?
   - How did you maintain a good relationship with the PM?

3. **How do you evaluate whether a feature request is worth building?**
   - What criteria do you use?
   - How do you push back on stakeholders when needed?

4. **Describe your approach to building MVPs vs. building for scale.**
   - How do you decide what "good enough" looks like?
   - When have you made the wrong call on this?

## Product-Engineering Collaboration

5. **How do you work with Product Managers to define requirements?**

   - How do you handle unclear or incomplete requirements?

6. **Tell me about a time when engineering constraints significantly impacted product scope.**

   - What was the final outcome?

7. **How do you involve your team in product discussions and decision-making?**
   - When do you include them vs. shield them from product debates?

8. **Describe a situation where you had to advocate for engineering needs (tech debt, refactoring, infrastructure) against feature pressure.**

12. **Describe a feature you built that had significant customer impact.**

---

## Tips for Answering

- **Use STAR format** (Situation, Task, Action, Result)
- **Show business thinking** - connect technical decisions to customer/business outcomes
- **Demonstrate collaboration** - emphasize partnership with product, not adversarial relationships
- **Be humble** - acknowledge mistakes and learning
- **Show startup aptitude** - comfort with ambiguity, speed, scrappiness
- **Quantify impact** - use metrics and specific outcomes when possible
- **Show customer empathy** - demonstrate you care about end users, not just code

## Question Strategy

### Prioritize Based on Who You're Talking To

**If Interviewer is Product Leader:**
- Focus on: Product strategy, vision, PM-EM collaboration, customer understanding
- Ask: #1-8, #24-27, #31, #33, #35, #43-45 (Tilt-specific)

**If Interviewer is Engineering Leader:**
- Focus on: Team structure, technical challenges, process, EM role expectations
- Ask: #9-12, #16-23, #32, #38, #42 (Tilt-specific)

**If Interviewer is Founder/CEO:**
- Focus on: Company vision, market, funding, biggest challenges
- Ask: #1-4, #13-15, #28-30, #31, #35, #37, #43-45 (Tilt-specific)

**If Interviewer is Another EM/Peer:**
- Focus on: Day-to-day, culture, what it's really like, honest feedback
- Ask: #10-12, #18-19, #29-30, #38, #42 (Tilt-specific)

**Must-Ask Tilt Questions (regardless of interviewer):**
- #32 (Real-time underwriting model - core innovation)
- #37 (Competitive advantage)
- #43 (Responsible lending - shows you understand the ethics)
- At least one technical question (#38 or #42)

### What Makes a Question Great

- **Shows you've done research** - reference something specific about Tilt
- **Demonstrates strategic thinking** - not just tactical questions
- **Can't be answered by website** - go deeper than public information
- **Opens dialogue** - invites storytelling, not yes/no answers
- **Reveals fit** - helps you evaluate if this is right for you

### Red Flags to Listen For

- **Vague answers about product strategy** - suggests lack of clarity
- **Defensiveness about technical debt** - culture issue
- **"We move too fast for process"** - chaos, not speed
- **Unclear success metrics** - flying blind
- **"We're family"** - boundary issues
- **Can't articulate differentiation** - market risk

**Tilt-Specific Red Flags:**
- **Vague on underwriting model** - their core innovation, should be crystal clear
- **Dismissive about regulatory compliance** - fintech is heavily regulated, CFPB oversight
- **Can't articulate responsible lending practices** - ethical red flag serving vulnerable population
- **Unclear on fraud/risk management** - critical at 5M+ customer scale
- **No clear moat beyond "AI"** - commoditized space with many competitors
- **Defensive about default rates or customer outcomes** - suggests problems
- **Purely growth-focused without unit economics** - unsustainable
- **Manual underwriting at scale** - won't work, tech should be automated

### Green Flags to Listen For

- **Specific customer examples** - knows their users
- **Honest about challenges** - transparent culture
- **Clear metrics** - data-driven
- **Thoughtful about quality** - not just speed
- **Engineers involved in product** - good collaboration
- **Excited about problems** - passionate team

**Tilt-Specific Green Flags:**
- **Clear articulation of underwriting model** - explains how real-time data beats credit scores
- **Data-driven on customer outcomes** - tracks credit score improvements, financial health
- **Balanced growth + responsibility** - thoughtful about serving vulnerable customers ethically
- **Strong compliance posture** - proactive about regulations, transparent about state limitations
- **Sophisticated fraud/risk strategy** - ML models, real-time monitoring at scale
- **Clear competitive moat** - proprietary data, models, or network effects beyond generic "AI"
- **Healthy unit economics** - LTV > CAC, sustainable business model
- **Technical depth** - real engineering challenges (ML, scale, real-time processing), not just CRUD
- **Mission-driven culture** - genuinely cares about financial inclusion, not predatory
- **Transparent about challenges** - honest about defaults, fraud, regulatory hurdles
